Traditional Clothing

Seana Marena

A traditional Basotho blanket. It is considered prestigious, and is traditionally worn by Basotho chiefs, elders and royalty.

Thethana

A traditional Basotho skirt made of fibre and beads. It is worn by young Basotho girls.

Seshoeshoe

A traditional dress worn by Basotho women. It is made from printed cotton fabric.

Khiba

A printed cotton skirt traditionally worn Basotho women. It is practically worn for outdoor activities.

Mokhahla

A traditional leather garment. It is made from animal skin or leather.

Leqapha

An attire worn by initiated Basotho men.

Sefatla

Traditional Basotho shoes made from animal skin.

Letlama

A Basotho blanket, usually worn by Basotho artists.

Kuoane

A traditional hat made from animal skin.

Mose oa khomo

Traditional leather dress, usually worn by Basotho female adults.

Mohapelo

A cloth worn around a women's head. A symbol for respect and modesty among Basotho women.

Mothiboloko

Beads worn around the waist by women. A symbol of feminity, beauty and marital status.

Likoena Blanket

A blanket decorated with crocodile motifs. It symbolizes strength and bravery.

Lihiea

A decorative shawl worn around the shoulders. Adds elegance and warmth, often worn by older women.
