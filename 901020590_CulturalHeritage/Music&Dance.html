Traditional Dances and Instruments

1. Mokhibo

A traditional dance performed by women kneeling, with shoulder and upper-body rhythmic movements.

2. Mohobelo

A man's stomp dance showing strength and rhythm, linked to worrior traditions.

3. Liphotha

A dance performed ny initiates during initiation ceremonies, often secretive and symbolic.

4. Motebelo

A group dance for women involving slow, graceful movements and singing.

5. Leloko

A social dance for both genders, often performed at weddings or community gatherings.

6. Seilatsatsi

A joyful celebratory dance done during traditional feasts and harvests.

7. Boroboro

A dance involving a mix of foot stamping and clapping in unison.

8. Mokorotlo Dance

A dance performed while wearing the Mokorotlo hat, representing pride and tradition.

9. Tlhakants'uke

A mixed-gender dance involving alternating moves between men and women.

10. Setolotolo

Amouth harp producing deep vibrating sounds, played with a stick and mouth resonance.

11. Lekope

A traditional whistle madde from reed, used to lead songs or alert others.

12. Moropa

A traditional drum made from animal skin and wood, central to dance rhythm.

13. Thomo

A bow-like string instrument used by women, played by striking the string with a stick.

14. Lenaka

A horn instrument made from animal horn, producing loud sounds for announcements or ceremonies.

15. Phalafala

A long trumpet-like horn blown during special events or to call gatherings.

16. Khapane

A shaker instrument made from gourds filled with seeds or stones.

17. Pere

A stinged instrument made tin and wire, played like a guitar.

18. Ntomo

A small percussion instrument used by children and dancers for rhythm.

19. Sekhankula

A traditional string instrument producing high-pitched melodic tunes.

20. Litema songs

Songs sung during the decorative practice of wall art, often by women.

21. Litolobonya

Songs performed by young women during come-of-age ceremonies.

22. Lithoko

Praise poetry recited rhymically, often accompanying dances or ceremonies.

22. Lekolulo

A flute traditionally played by herding boys.
